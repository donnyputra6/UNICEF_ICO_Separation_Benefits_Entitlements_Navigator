What You Need to Do on Separation due to Post Abolishment
Follow these steps to manage your separation process
01
Abolishment Notification Received
Key action: Acknowledge your notification, review your options, and check if you qualify to sign the No Contest Agreement.
  • Acknowledge the abolishment notification.
  • If your post is abolished on your contract end date and you have at least 5 years of continuous service, consider signing the No Contest Agreement – please contact your People & Culture Business Partner for more information. Eligible staff: FT, Continuing, and Permanent appointment holders only. Benefit: separation payment in lieu of termination indemnity. Signing is voluntary but time-bound — act within the timeframe in your notification.
  • You are prioritized in recruitments until the post abolishment date — keep an eye on suitable vacancies and keep applying.
  • To estimate your separation package, use the Separation Calculator. Final entitlements will be confirmed by GSSC at closure of your case.
  • Run pension estimates on the UNJSPF Member Self-Service.
  • If you have outstanding debts (salary advance, rental advance, etc.), review your repayment plan and revise it with your People & Culture Business Partner and/or GSSC Payroll as necessary.
  • If you have an outstanding Education Grant advance, submit the claim at least one month before separation — otherwise the full amount will be recovered from your last salary and/or final pay. Refer to the Education Grant Knowledge Article.
02
Between Notification and Two Months Before Separation
Key action: Consider SLWOP or Temporary Assignment options, and plan your remaining leave before the ceiling kicks in.
  • Consider going on Special Leave Without Pay (SLWOP) for up to 12 months — you remain an internal candidate for vacancies during this period. Note: Requires office approval; not automatic. SLWOP does not count as active service for pension or entitlements. UNICEF health insurance coverage is suspended during SLWOP. Any outside work or employment during SLWOP requires prior approval from the Representative (Outside Activities policy). Contact your People & Culture Business Partner to discuss eligibility.
  • Fixed-Term/Permanent/Continuing appointment holders: Consider a Temporary Assignment to retain your FT entitlements instead of separating. Note: Must be within UNICEF; does not halt the abolishment timeline; FT entitlements retained for TA duration only. Confirm eligibility with your People & Culture Business Partner.
  • Carefully plan and discuss leaves and flexible working arrangements with your supervisor and local HR.
  • Utilise annual leave days beyond the ceiling (60 days for FT/Perm/Continuing; 18 days for TA) — any days above the maximum will be forfeited.
03
One Month Prior to the Abolishment Date
Key action: Submit your Collect Input task in Service Gateway with all required documents, and ensure all leaves are approved 20 days before your last day.

Make sure all outstanding amounts are cleared or will be cleared before your last day under contract.

Fill in the Collect Input task received via Service Gateway and attach the necessary documentation:

  • Your latest valid passport/national ID
  • Your completed Pension form
  • Proof of banking for UNJSPF payment purposes
  • Retirees: Birth certificates or passports/national IDs of your recorded dependents, your marriage/divorce certificate if applicable
  • After Service Health Insurance (ASHI) enrolment form if eligible (optional). Please find more information on the ASHI eligibility in the related knowledge article in Service Gateway.

Please make sure that all your leaves and attendances for the remainder of your contract are submitted and approved 20 days prior to your last day under contract.

Complete Performance Evaluation Report (PER) for yourself and your supervisees.

04
On Your Last Day of the Contract
Key action: Return all office equipment, clear outstanding amounts, and download your certificate of employment and payslips before system access is revoked.

Return your office equipment and UNLP, building pass, mobile phone, etc.

Clear any pending outstanding amounts towards UNICEF.

Make sure all your leaves and attendances are up to date.

Download a certificate of employment in Service Gateway and your payslips from Fiori (Paystub menu) before your system accesses are revoked.

05
For IP Staff Eligible for Repatriation Grant
Key action: Send proof of residency in your new place of residence to separatedstaff@unicef.org once you have left your duty station.

Once you have left the country of your duty station, provide proof of residency in your new place of residence and send it to separatedstaff@unicef.org. Remember to specify your full name and UNICEF personnel number for ease of reference.

For more information on claiming your repatriation grant, please refer to Repatriation Grant for more guidance.

06
Approximately One Month Following Your Separation Date
Key action: Expect your final pay 4–6 weeks after separation. For any queries, contact GSSC at separatedstaff@unicef.org with your full name and personnel number.

Your final pay (separation package) will arrive to the bank account selected.

In case of any questions/queries/requests, please contact GSSC HR Offboarding colleagues at separatedstaff@unicef.org. Remember to specify your full name and UNICEF personnel number for ease of reference.

Frequently Asked Questions — General separation entitlements
LeaveWill I receive compensation for all the annual leave days I did not use?
▾

Upon separation staff will receive compensation of their unused annual leave days. The maximum will depend on your contract type:

  • Fixed-term (FT): Max. 60 days. If you had a previous FT/Continuing/Permanent contract with a break of max. 12 months, the previous payout and current payout combined cannot exceed 60 days.
  • Continuing/Permanent (CONT/PERM): Max. 60 days. Same cumulative rule applies if a previous FT/Cont/Perm contract existed within a 12-month break.
  • Temporary Appointment: Max. 18 days. Previous payouts under other contract types are not counted.

*Previous separation applies when you separated and were reappointed within 12 months. For example: separated from a fixed-term contract on 31 January 2012 and reappointed on 01 April 2012 — annual leave balance paid out across both separations cannot exceed 60 days.

LeaveHow can I check if I had annual leave payout before?
▾

You may download your last payslip of your previous contract — it will contain the breakdown of the payments.

  • If you were previously separated from UNICEF, you may ask for this breakdown during your current separation process.
  • If you were previously separated from another UN agency, you may contact the HR colleagues from that agency.
LeaveIs it allowed to take annual leave in the last month before separation?
▾

Yes, subject to approval from your supervisor.

LeaveHow much is one day of unused annual leave? Is there any way to calculate it?
▾

One unused annual leave day equals to one day of gross salary minus staff assessment. You will find the amounts in your payslip and you may also use the Separation Calculator for a very close estimate.

LeaveWhat happens with the unused annual leaves if I secure a new post? Will these still be paid or will they be carried over to the new assignment?
▾

Unused annual leave days are paid out only if a separation happens. In case you secure a new post without separating from service, there will be no payout and you will keep your annual leave balance.

LeaveI worked with another UN agency before on a Fixed Term contract and received 60 days of unused annual leave balance. Now I am on a Temporary Appointment with UNICEF. Will I be paid any annual leave balance?
▾

Yes, as you hold a Temporary Appointment this time, the previously received annual leave balance will not be taken into consideration, and you may receive an annual leave balance payout up to the maximum of 18 days.

LeaveAt the time of my previous separation I have been paid 5 days annual leave balance. Now I have 10 days of unused annual leave balance. Does this mean that I will only get paid for 5 days?
▾

No, the two balances together are still below the maximum of 60 days, therefore you will be paid 10 days of unused annual leave balance.

LeaveWill the previously paid annual leave days be deducted if I separated from a Temporary Appointment and now I am holding another Temporary Appointment?
▾

No, in this case you are again entitled to a maximum of 18 days of unused annual leave balance.

LeaveThis is my first appointment and my annual leave balance is beyond 60 days. What happens to the excess of 10 days?
▾

Any annual leave beyond 60 days will be forfeited. You may wish to take these annual leaves before separation, if approved by your supervisor.

LeaveAt the time of my previous separation from a Fixed Term contract I received 60 days. Now I am on a new Fixed Term appointment but the break between the two contracts was more than a year. Will I receive any annual leave balance now?
▾

If the break in service between the two Fixed Term contracts was longer than a year, you may again receive the maximum of 60 days of unused annual leave balance.

LeaveHow and when will I receive my unused annual leave compensation?
▾

Upon separation you will receive a "final payment" which will contain all the amounts due to you upon separation. Your annual leave balance (if any) is paid automatically; you will not need to claim it. The final payment is released about 4-6 weeks after your separation date. In case of any dispute, UN Staff Rules and Regulations and UNICEF policies/procedures will prevail.

CTOWho is entitled to unused CTO compensation?
▾

Only general service staff can have CTO during their employment, therefore upon separation only General Service staff can have a cash compensation of their unused CTO. Colleagues from the International Professional or National Officer category do not accrue CTO.

CTOHow much unused CTO can be compensated upon separation?
▾

Upon separation 40 CTO hours can be paid plus the CTO accrued during the last 4 months of employment. CTO in excess will be forfeited. To avoid loss of your CTO hours beyond this limit, please plan and use your CTO before your separation.

CTOHow and when will I receive my CTO compensation payment?
▾

Upon separation you will receive a "final payment" which will contain all the amounts due to you upon separation. CTO (if any) is paid automatically; you will not need to claim it. The final payment is released about 4-6 weeks after your separation date. In case of any dispute, UN Staff Rules and Regulations and UNICEF policies/procedures will prevail.

NoticeWhat is the in lieu of notice payment?
▾
  • Fixed-term, Permanent or Continuing appointment: You will receive a written notification 6 months prior to the effective date of abolishment. If this is not possible, compensation equivalent to salary, post adjustment and allowances for the remaining notice period will be paid to you — this is called in lieu of notice compensation. Example: if only 4 months of notice is given, you will receive 2 months of in lieu of notice compensation.
  • Temporary Appointment: You will receive a written notification 15 days prior to the effective date of abolishment. If this is not possible, compensation equivalent to salary, post adjustment and allowances will be paid. Example: if only 10 days of notice is given, you will receive 5 days of in lieu of notice compensation.
NoticeI am holding a Temporary Appointment. When do I receive an in lieu of notice payment?
▾

Staff members holding a temporary appointment will receive a written notification about the abolishment 15 days prior to the effective date of abolishment. If this is not possible, compensation equivalent to salary, post adjustment and allowances will be paid to the staff member. E.g. if only 10 days of notice is given before the abolishment, the staff member will receive 5 days of in lieu of notice compensation.

NoticeDo I need to claim in lieu of notice payment?
▾

No, the GSSC Offboarding will see when the written notification was sent to you. If an in lieu of payment is due to you, it will be paid to you as part of your final payment.

NoticeCan I ask for delayed separation instead of in lieu of notice compensation?
▾

No, the delayed abolishment notification will have no impact on your separation date. The in lieu of notice is always a cash compensation. In case of any dispute, UN Staff Rules and Regulations and UNICEF policies/procedures will prevail.

Frequently Asked Questions — IP separation entitlements
TravelWho is entitled to repatriation travel and shipment?
▾

International professional staff members serving outside their home country and country of nationality are entitled to repatriation travel and shipment upon separation, if they were appointed there at UNICEF's cost. Eligible family members' (spouse and dependent children) also have a travel and shipment entitlement, if they were installed to the duty station at UNICEF's cost. Family members' travel expenses are not covered if: you are holding a temporary appointment OR you are serving a duty station classified as non-family duty station.

TravelCan I travel to any destination?
▾
  • Temporary Appointment: Liability is from your duty station to your place of recruitment. You may travel to an alternative destination up to the cost of the travel liability.
  • Fixed-term, Continuing or Permanent contract: Liability is from your duty station to your place of recruitment or place of home leave. You may travel to an alternative destination up to the cost of the travel liability.
TravelHow will my repatriation travel be arranged?
▾

If you are entitled to repatriation travel, you will have 2 options:

  • Option 1 — Travel arranged by UNICEF: Your office will arrange tickets through the most direct and economic route, with 25 kg excess baggage per person.
  • Option 2 — Travel lump sum: 75% of the lowest unrestrictive airfare according to UNICEF liability. You must request this option before your travel takes place.
TravelCan I lose my repatriation travel entitlement?
▾

Yes, you may lose your repatriation travel entitlement in the below cases: you separate within 6 months after the return date from your last home leave (shipment entitlement may be granted); you have commenced your last R&R less than 31 days prior to your separation date (shipment entitlement may be granted); if you do not start your repatriation travel within two years after the date of separation (shipment entitlement is also lost).

TravelMay I have my repatriation travel later?
▾

Yes, if you are entitled to repatriation travel, you may take it any time within 2 years after your separation date.

TravelWhat is my shipment entitlement?
▾

Temporary Appointment:

  • Option 1 — Unaccompanied shipment: 100 kg by air + shipment insurance USD 1,600.
  • Option 2 — Relocation Grant in lieu: USD 1,500.

Fixed-term (except JPO), Continuing or Permanent:

  • Option 1 — Unaccompanied shipment / Removal of household goods:
    • Without spouse/dependent child: standard 20-foot container (33.2 cubic metres) + shipment insurance USD 16,000.
    • With spouse/dependent child at duty station: standard 40-foot container (67.7 cubic metres) + shipment insurance USD 16,000.
  • Option 2 — Relocation Grant in lieu: USD 13,000 (travelling alone); USD 18,000 (travelling with eligible family members).

Junior Professional Officer (JPO):

  • Option 1 — Unaccompanied shipment:
    • Staff member: 600 kg
    • First eligible family member: 250 kg
    • Every other eligible family member: 150 kg
    • Shipment insurance: USD 16,000
  • Option 2 — Relocation Grant in lieu: USD 9,000 (travelling alone); USD 12,000 (travelling with eligible family members).
GrantWhat is the repatriation grant?
▾

The repatriation grant is paid to ex-patriate staff members to assist their re-establishment in a country other than the country of the last duty station, if they meet the eligibility conditions. This is a one-time lump sum payment at the time of the separation.

GrantWho is entitled to a repatriation grant?
▾

The repatriation grant is payable to International Professional staff members who leave the country of the last duty station when separating. You are entitled if all of the following conditions are met:

  • You have completed at least five years of qualifying service (cumulative service since your most recent appointment or reappointment, served outside your country of nationality, home leave and permanent residence).*
  • At the time of separation, you are residing outside your country of nationality, country of home leave or country of permanent residence.
  • You relocate away from the country of your last duty station.

*The following do not count as qualifying service: (a) special leave without pay longer than 31 days, and (b) periods served in the home country, country of nationality or country of permanent residence.

GrantWill repatriation grant be automatically paid?
▾

No, if you are entitled to repatriation grant payment, you will need to relocate outside your last duty station within 2 years after your separation date and submit a repatriation grant claim. The claim should include documents to prove that you have established residence in a country other than that of the last duty station. If not claimed within 2 years after the separation's effective date, the repatriation grant entitlement will be lost.

GrantI am teleworking from my home country. Does it impact my repatriation grant?
▾

If you have been teleworking in your home country, country of nationality, or country of permanent residence on TODS 2 for six months or longer at the time or within one calendar month of separation: you will be considered to be residing in the country and are not eligible for repatriation grant. You may regain eligibility once you have spent one calendar month at the duty station prior to separation (and have the minimum qualifying service as outlined in paragraph 93 of UNICEF procedure on separation from service DHR/PROCEDURE/2022/002, as amended).

GrantCan I claim repatriation grant if I have permanent residence status at the last duty station?
▾

No, the repatriation grant will not be payable.

GrantMy spouse is also a UN staff member. Does it have an impact on my repatriation grant entitlement?
▾
  • If your spouse is not entitled to repatriation grant: This does not have an impact on your repatriation grant entitlement.
  • If your spouse is also entitled to repatriation grant: The first parent to separate may claim at the rate applicable to a staff member with a spouse or dependent child. The second parent, if also eligible, may claim either:
    • At the rate applicable for a staff member with neither a spouse nor dependent child — for the period of service subsequent to the separation of the spouse; or
    • At the rate applicable for a staff member with a spouse or dependent child — for the whole period of qualifying service, less the amount paid to the first parent.

See paragraphs 95 and 96 in DHR PROCEDURE 2022/002 v. 24 July 2023.

Frequently Asked Questions — Termination indemnity & separation payment
IndemnityMy post is abolished. Will I receive termination indemnity?
▾

Staff whose post is abolished before their contract expiry date will receive a termination indemnity. If your post is abolished on your contract expiry date, you may receive a separation payment (see the description below).

IndemnityHow is the termination indemnity calculated?
▾

If you have less than 6 years of continuous service:

  • 1–6 months of uncompleted service: 6 weeks
  • 7–12 months of uncompleted service: 1 week for each uncompleted month
  • 13 months or more of uncompleted service: 3 months

If you have 6 or more years of continuous service (as per Annex 1):

  • 6 completed years: 3 months (FT) / 6 months (Continuing)
  • 7 completed years: 5 months / 7 months
  • 8 completed years: 7 months / 8 months
  • 9 completed years: 9 months / 9 months
  • 10 completed years: 9.5 months / 9.5 months
  • 11 completed years: 10 months / 10 months
  • 12 completed years: 10.5 months / 10.5 months
  • 13 completed years: 11 months / 11 months
  • 14 completed years: 11.5 months / 11.5 months
  • 15 or more completed years: 12 months / 12 months

*Service period = continuous service since your latest appointment or reappointment, excluding special leave without pay periods longer than 31 days.

IndemnityMy contract expires and my post is abolished the same day. Will I receive termination indemnity?
▾

You will not receive termination indemnity. However, if you meet the below criteria, you may receive "separation payment" (equivalent to the Termination Indemnity): you have at least 5 years of continuous fixed-term service completed since your most recent appointment or reappointment; and you sign the No Contest Agreement.

IndemnityCan I have the 50% additional termination indemnity?
▾
  • Notification received before 01 April 2025: You may receive an additional 50% termination indemnity by signing the No Contest Agreement. Signing is optional and means you withdraw pending claims and appeals against UNICEF and will not file new ones.
  • If you do not sign the No Contest Agreement: You will receive the regular termination indemnity (without the additional 50%).
  • If you opt for SLWOP starting from the effective date of the post abolishment or termination: You will not be entitled to the additional 50% termination indemnity.
  • Notification received on or after 01 April 2025: You are not eligible to receive the additional 50% termination indemnity.
IndemnityWhen will I receive my termination indemnity?
▾

Upon separation you will receive a "final payment" which will contain all the amounts due to you upon separation. The termination indemnity or separation payment (if applicable) is paid automatically; you will not need to claim it. The final payment is released about 4-6 weeks after your separation date.

IndemnityIs termination indemnity paid automatically at separation, or do I need to sign a No Contest Agreement?
▾

When the contract is valid beyond the abolishment date, termination indemnity is payable, without any No Contest Agreement. A No Contest Agreement is offered for consideration if: (a) the contract is expiring on the abolishment date and the staff member has at least 5 years of continuous service (No Contest Agreement for separation payment); (b) if the contract is a FT/Continuing/Permanent appointment and the abolishment notification was issued before 1 April 2025 (No Contest Agreement for additional 50% termination indemnity).

IndemnityWe haven't had a PBR yet, but we got separation letters, no decision on the post abolishment yet. Are we eligible for indemnity?
▾

Non-extension or termination of contract due to lack of funding is handled the same way as a post abolishment. A termination indemnity will apply for staff members whose contract is valid beyond the lack of funding effective date irrespective of length of service; separation payment will apply for staff members whose contract expires on the lack of funding effective date but have at least 5 years of continuous service and have signed a No Contest Agreement for separation payment.

IndemnityHow does termination indemnity differ from separation payment?
▾

Termination indemnity applies when your contract is valid beyond the post abolishment date, regardless of the length of service. Separation payment applies when your post is abolished on your contract expiration date, you have at least 5 years of continuous service and you sign a No Contest Agreement for separation payment. Both termination indemnity and separation payment are calculated in the same manner: 1 month of termination indemnity/separation payment equals to 1 month of gross salary minus staff assessment.

IndemnityWhat is the actual calculation of termination indemnity if the continuous service is less than 6 years (Temporary Appointment and Fixed Term Appointment)?
▾

Each full month of uncompleted service becomes 1 week of termination indemnity. The minimum payable is 6 weeks, the maximum is 3 months of termination indemnity.

IndemnityI am confused, does one need 5 or 6 continuous years of service for separation payment?
▾
  • 5 years of continuous service is the threshold required when your contract is abolished on the contract end date (SAME scenario). You must also sign the No Contest Agreement to receive the separation payment.
  • When your contract is valid beyond the abolishment date (BEYOND scenario): Termination indemnity is payable in all cases, regardless of length of service.
  • Less than 6 years of service: Each month of uncompleted service = 1 week of termination indemnity. Minimum payable: 6 weeks. Maximum payable: 3 months.
  • 6 or more years of service: Termination indemnity is calculated based on Annex 1 of the Separation Policy. Minimum payable: 3 months. Maximum payable: 12 months.
IndemnityAre only the number of years of service or also the months taken into consideration for the termination indemnity calculation?
▾

Months are also part of the calculation, not only full years.

IndemnityIf you are a Fixed Term holder, your post is abolished but you accept a new offer before the abolishment date, are you still entitled to a separation package?
▾

No, in this case a reassignment will happen and the separation from service is no longer happening. Unless you separate from service and start a whole new contract — in this case the separation package will be paid.

IndemnityPlease define continuous service. I had several contracts with just 1 day of break in service in between. Do these contracts count towards my continuous service?
▾

Continuous service means service not interrupted by a separation. Each separation interrupts the service continuity and the count restarts every time you are reappointed. Your current continuous service is calculated from your last reappointment date.

IndemnityAre inter-agency moves such as secondment and transfer part of the continuous service count?
▾

Yes, if you joined UNICEF through an inter-agency transfer, your continuous service started with your previous agency, as no separation from service happens under an inter-agency transfer agreement. Inter-agency secondments are also included in the continuous service count.

Frequently Asked Questions — Pension
PensionWhen may I retire?
▾

Normal retirement — requires at least 5 years of pension contributions and reaching normal retirement age:

  • Age 60 — if you started pension contributions before 01 January 1990
  • Age 62 — if you started between 01 January 1990 and 31 December 2013
  • Age 65 — if you started on or after 01 January 2014

Early retirement — you may retire any time between your early and normal retirement age, with at least 5 years of contributions. Early retirement age is:

  • Age 55 — if contributions started before 01 January 2014
  • Age 58 — if contributions started on or after 01 January 2014
PensionMy normal retirement age is 62 years. May I work until I turn 65 years?
▾

Yes, it is your personal choice whether you want to retire at your normal retirement age. If you have a contract beyond your normal retirement age and you do not want to retire, you may work until you turn 65 years.

PensionMy normal retirement age is 62 years. May I retire at the age of 63 years?
▾

Yes, this is possible. You may retire if you have at least 5 years of pension contributions and have already reached your normal retirement age.

PensionI have a contract until I turn 65 years old. May I retire earlier?
▾
  • Normal retirement age 60: You may retire any time between age 60 and 65, provided you have at least 5 years of contributions.
  • Normal retirement age 62: You may retire any time between age 62 and 65, provided you have at least 5 years of contributions.

To initiate retirement, submit a "notice to retire" to your office:

  • Fixed-term contract: 1 month before your planned retirement date
  • Continuing/Permanent contract: 3 months before
  • Temporary appointment: 15 days before
PensionMy post is abolished, and I have already reached my retirement age. May I retire?
▾

Yes, if you separate on post abolishment and you are at your normal retirement age already and have at least 5 years of pension contribution, you may claim a monthly retirement benefit from the Pension Fund. Please note that to avoid double compensation, the period of termination indemnity may not exceed the period before mandatory retirement age (65).

PensionWhat are the pension benefits?
▾

Pension benefits are only available upon separation. Benefits depend on your contribution period and age:

  • Less than 5 years — any age: Withdrawal settlement (deferrable up to 36 months). Use the PENS E.6 form (with video tutorial).
  • At least 5 years — below early retirement age: Withdrawal settlement or Deferred Retirement Benefit (deferrable up to 36 months). Use the PENS E/7 form (with tutorial).
  • At least 5 years — between early and normal retirement age: Withdrawal settlement, Deferred Retirement Benefit (deferrable up to 36 months), or Early Retirement Benefit.
  • At least 5 years — at normal retirement age: Normal Retirement Benefit.
PensionWhen and where to submit the pension documents?
▾

You will receive a "collect input" link from Offboarding (in email) and you will need to submit scanned copies of the pension documents there. Original copies are not requested; however you must retain them for 10 years. No need to send the pension documents to the GSSC before your separation case is opened.

PensionHow much will my pension benefit be?
▾

Pension benefits are calculated and paid by the Pension Fund directly. You may check your future benefit estimates in the Pension Fund's Member Self-Service. How to register: How-to-Register-in-Member-Self-Service.pdf. How to see your benefit estimates: Benefit Estimates — UNJSPF.

PensionHow long does it take to receive my pension?
▾

GSSC will review and submit your pension documents to the Pension Fund and send you an email notification (from the "GSSC Pension Forms" inbox). This usually happens close to your separation date. If all the documents are in order and you have no debts (to UNICEF, UNFCU, etc.), the Pension Fund will release your pension benefit about 4-6 weeks after the receipt of your last month salary. Should you have questions after the documents are submitted to the Pension Fund, you may contact the Pension Fund directly: Contact Us — UNJSPF.

PensionCan I have my pension benefits without separation?
▾

No, the pension benefit is only payable to staff separated from a UN agency.

PensionI am at my early retirement age and my post is abolished. Do I need to choose between separation due to post abolishment or separation due to early retirement?
▾

A staff member whose post is abolished and is also entitled to pension benefits at the same time (early retirement benefits in this case) will receive the abolishment-related separation package and may also opt for pension benefits in the pension form.

PensionCan I receive both termination indemnity and pension for an overlapping period?
▾

Yes, until the age of 65. Once you turn 65, only pension payment may apply.

Frequently Asked Questions — ASHI & ASLI
ASHIWhat is ASHI?
▾

While employed at UNICEF, every staff member is entitled to be covered by a United Nations Health Insurance Plan. Upon separation, eligible staff members may apply for an After-Service Health Insurance (ASHI). The ASHI is available only as a continuation, without interruption between active service and retirement status.

ASHIHow do I know whether I am eligible for ASHI?
▾

You will be eligible to ASHI if you meet each of the criteria below: you have a permanent, continuing or fixed-term appointment at the time of your separation; you have contributed to a United Nations Health Insurance Plan for a minimum of 10 years (both continuous OR cumulative contribution may be considered — however, participation during temporary appointment is not considered); you have already reached your early retirement age; you will receive a monthly benefit from the Pension Fund after the separation (early or normal retirement benefit); you apply for ASHI within the deadline.

ASHIWhat if I have less than 10 years of cumulative contribution to a UN health plan under an eligible contract? May I still benefit from ASHI?
▾

In case you started contributing to a UN health plan before July 2007, 5 years of cumulative participation are sufficient. However, you need to be separating from an eligible contract: Fixed Term/Permanent/Continuing.

ASHIMay I enroll my family members to ASHI?
▾

You may enroll: your spouse (if enrolled in your medical insurance plan at the time of separation with a total enrolment period of 5 years) and children until the age of 25 (if enrolled in your medical insurance plan at the time of separation with a total enrolment period of 5 years). You may decide whether you enroll each of them, some of them or none of them.

ASHIHow and when may I apply for ASHI?
▾

Application is possible during the separation process. The GSSC will send you a separation note with a link to the ASHI application form. You will need to fill and sign this form and submit a scanned copy in Service Gateway (via the "collect input" link that will be provided to you in email). Application deadline: you must submit your ASHI application form 31 days prior to separation and no later than 31 days from the date of separation. Late applications are not accepted.

ASHIHow much are the ASHI costs and how will I pay them?
▾
  • Local staff (General Service or National Officer): ASHI monthly premium will be approximately 50% of the MIP costs.
  • International Professional or Director staff: Please consult the ASHI subsidy and contribution rates chart on the UN Health and Life Insurance Section's website.

Your ASHI contributions will be deducted from your monthly retirement benefit — no action required from you.

ASHIHow long is the ASHI enrolment process?
▾

Your ASHI plan will be activated as of the first day of the month following your separation (e.g. if you separate on 25 June 2025, your UNICEF health insurance will be active until 30 June 2025; your ASHI plan's effective date is 01 July 2025). However, in some cases the administration may be lengthy (several weeks). This has no impact on the effective date of your ASHI plan, but there might be some delay in claim reimbursement during the first weeks.

ASHIIs ASHI mandatory? What if I am not interested?
▾

No, ASHI enrolment is not mandatory. If you are eligible, but do not want to activate your plan, you should let the GSSC know during your separation process. Please note, however, that if you do not activate your ASHI plan upon separation, the ASHI plan enrolment is not allowed later.

ASHIMy spouse is also a UN staff member. Will it impact my ASHI eligibility?
▾

Please make sure you highlight this during the separation process. If your spouse is still an active staff member at a UN agency (with a fixed-term, continuing or permanent contract), you should be enrolled to their medical plan after your separation. However, at the same time, if you would like to have an ASHI plan, remember to submit your ASHI application at the time of your separation. When both of you are separated, one of you will be the ASHI subscriber and the other will be enrolled as a dependent spouse in their plan OR you may have individual plans.

ASHII am opting for a deferred retirement benefit. Can I take ASHI?
▾

Yes, if you are eligible to ASHI at the time of separation. In this case you will need to pay your ASHI contributions directly, until you are in receipt of a monthly pension benefit. At that point the ASHI premium will be deducted from your pension benefit.

ASHICan ASHI coverage be terminated at any point in time? What is the procedure?
▾

Yes, the plan can be terminated at any point, but the former staff member cannot return to ASHI anymore. Former IP staff should turn to HLIS for terminating the plan; former local staff should reach out to GSSC HR Offboarding via [email protected]. In case of any dispute, SRR and UNICEF policies/procedures will prevail.

ASLIWhat is ASLI? Who is eligible?
▾

The Hartford Group Life Insurance (GLI) is a voluntary plan that is not subsidized by UNICEF and is not mandatory for staff members. You are eligible for automatic participation in the After-Service Life Insurance (ASLI) if you: are at least 55 years old at the time of your separation; participated in Group Life Insurance for at least ten years as an active staff member; have an active life insurance plan at the time of your separation. After-Service Life Insurance eligibility is determined by the UN Health and Life Insurance Section located in UNHQ. If you are eligible, coverage is automatic and requires no application. Life insurance plan participants with at least 10 years of contributory participation under the age of 55 on separation are eligible for one-year free life insurance coverage at the level of coverage on the date of separation for each completed ten years of participation. Please visit the UN Health and Life Insurance Section website for further information and reach out to [email protected].

Frequently Asked Questions — Early separation (separation before the post abolishment date)
SeparationMy post is abolished in December 2025. May I separate earlier?
▾

Yes, this is possible and this is called "early separation". If you wish to leave before the abolishment date, send a written request to your supervisor with the following notice period:

  • Fixed-term contract: 1 month before your planned separation
  • Continuing/Permanent contract: 3 months before your planned separation
  • Temporary appointment: 15 days before your planned separation

Important: You must request "early separation" — do not submit your request as a "resignation", as this will result in loss of your separation entitlements.

SeparationWill my early separation automatically be granted?
▾

No, this must first be approved by the Head of Office. The Head of Office will decide on the request, taking into account the needs of the organization, the supervisor's recommendation, and your wishes. If approved, you may separate on post abolishment before the abolishment date.

SeparationIs early separation different from resignation?
▾

Yes, early separation is not a resignation. If you resign, you will lose any termination indemnity or separation payment that may be due to you upon separation on post abolishment. To ensure you separate on post abolishment, please ask for "early separation" and do not resign.

SeparationWill I lose my termination indemnity if I ask for early separation?
▾

No, if early separation is approved, you will separate on post abolishment and your entitlements will be:

  • Salary (with post adjustment, allowances) paid until your separation date
  • Separation entitlements (including termination indemnity and separation payment), as applicable
SeparationWill an early separation affect the amount of my termination indemnity?
▾
  • Notification received before 1 April 2025: Your early separation will not affect the amount of your termination indemnity. It will be calculated as if you remained in service until the post abolishment date.
  • Notification received on or after 1 April 2025: The termination indemnity will be calculated based on your continuous active service until your actual (early) separation date.
Frequently Asked Questions — SLWOP and temporary assignment
SLWOPWho is eligible to take SLWOP?
▾

Staff who on or before 31 December 2025, receive notice of termination of their appointment, may request to be placed on SLWOP. The application must be submitted to your country Office as soon as possible, but no later than one month before the effective date of the termination. Staff who apply for SLWOP may not receive the 50% additional termination indemnity.

SLWOPI already signed the agreement to receive the 50% additional termination indemnity. May I still apply for SLWOP?
▾

If you already signed the no-contest agreement to receive the 50% termination indemnity, to become eligible for SLWOP for 12 months you need to revoke the no-contest agreement signed.

SLWOPWhen may I start my SLWOP?
▾

You may start the SLWOP from the effective date of termination.

SLWOPHow many months in advance should I request the SLWOP?
▾

At least two months before the SLWOP start date.

SLWOPWhat is the maximum duration on this SLWOP?
▾

The maximum duration of the SLWOP is 12 months.

SLWOPHow many months of SLWOP may I request at one time?
▾

You may apply for SLWOP for a minimum of 3 months and a maximum of 12 months after abolition of post. Only one extension may be requested, provided that the total SLWOP period does not exceed 12 months.

SLWOPWhat happens if my SLWOP for 12 months already started but I wish to separate from service before the approved end date?
▾

If a staff member separates before the end of their SLWOP after abolishment, this will be considered a curtailment of the SLWOP under the special support measures of FFI, not a resignation. The staff member will thus remain eligible for termination indemnity. This approach will remain in place until the special support measures are officially discontinued.

SLWOPWill I have health insurance coverage during SLWOP?
▾

You may keep your health insurance plan active by paying both your own and the organization's share of the contribution in quarterly payments. Please check the contribution premiums in your payslip.

SLWOPCan I take SLWOP even if my service is less than 1 year long?
▾

If your post is abolished and your contract type is Fixed Term/Permanent/Continuing appointment, you may request SLWOP for 12 months as per the latest guidance.

SLWOPIf I am opting for SLWOP, what entitlements will I receive immediately and what will be deferred until the separation date following the SLWOP?
▾

SLWOP does not come with any entitlements, and its purpose is to allow the staff member to keep applying for vacancies as an internal candidate and to avoid separating from the organization. Therefore, the separation-related entitlements will be released once the separation happened.

SLWOPCan I stay at the duty station during the SLWOP?
▾

You may stay at the duty station. However, UNICEF will not sponsor, support, or facilitate any visa or permit applications you may need.

SLWOPAm I allowed to engage in outside activity during the SLWOP?
▾

You must not engage in any outside employment, whether paid or unpaid, without first obtaining written approval from the relevant authority in accordance with the UNICEF Procedure on Outside Activities (Procedure/DHR/2021/007 v 23 May 2023, as amended).

SLWOPWhat if I do not find a new post within UNICEF before the end of the SLWOP?
▾

You will separate at the end of the 12-month period unless you are re-employed, reinstated, or selected for a temporary assignment. If you take up a temporary assignment after being on SLWOP, you are not eligible to request SLWOP again, regardless of the duration of the temporary assignment.

SLWOPWill I receive my termination indemnity at the end of my SLWOP?
▾

If you separate at the end of your SLWOP, you will receive all the final payments upon your separation from service due to abolishment/termination or non-extension of contract due to lack of funding.

SLWOPWill the SLWOP influence my separation entitlements?
▾

No, the same separation package will apply but it will only be released at the end of the SLWOP, upon separation.

SLWOPWhat is the advantage of taking SLWOP instead of separating directly?
▾

Yes, to maintain pension and/or health contribution for instance (optional). Another advantage is to apply for vacancies as an internal candidate during the SLWOP period.

SLWOPIf you are an IP, will you get the repatriation grant and relocation grant even if you are opting for the SLWOP?
▾

Yes, however the time spent on SLWOP will not count towards the qualifying service for the repatriation grant. Both the repatriation grant and relocation grant will be released after the SLWOP, at the time of separation, if applicable.

SLWOPWill I still be eligible for repatriation travel if I left the duty station for the SLWOP period?
▾

Yes, at the time of separation you will still be eligible for a repatriation travel. As SLWOP does not come with a travel entitlement, leaving the duty station at your own expense does not count as repatriation.

SLWOPIs it mandatory to use up all your annual leave days before SLWOP?
▾

No, it is not mandatory. Any unused annual leaves will be paid out to you in case of separation, up to the maximum days payable based on your contract type and service history.

SLWOPWill I still have access to my unicef.org email address to see the internal vacancy announcements during SLWOP?
▾

Yes. It is recommended that you log in at least once in two weeks in order to avoid the automatic deactivation of your accesses. In case the accesses become inactive, these can be reactivated with IT support. In case of any dispute, UN Staff Rules and Regulations and UNICEF policies/procedures will prevail.

TempWho is eligible to take on a temporary assignment?
▾

Staff considered on abolished post status who apply and are selected to a temporary appointment in the international professional category with a start date before 31 December 2025 may take on a temporary assignment. During the temporary assignment you will maintain your fixed-term status, but without lien on your abolished post.

TempWhat is the maximum duration of a temporary assignment?
▾
  • Non-hardship duty station: Maximum cumulative period of up to 364 days. At the end of the assignment you will separate, unless you are re-employed, reinstated, or placed on a successive temporary assignment (subject to the 364-day cumulative limit). Once you have cumulatively spent 364 days on temporary assignment, you are ineligible to request SLWOP.
  • Hardship duty station (L2/L3, classified "E"/"D" or Non-Family status): Maximum cumulative period of up to 729 days. You must complete six (6) months of service before applying to other posts or being reassigned.
TempCan all staff on an abolished post apply for a temporary assignment, regardless of the hardship category of the duty station?
▾

Staff members who received their abolishment notifications prior to 1 April 2025 may take on a temporary assignment to a hardship (L2/L3, classified "E"/"D" or Non-Family status) duty station without losing any of the separation entitlements. However, they can only take on a temporary assignment in a non-hardship duty station if they revoke their previously signed No Contest Agreement for additional 50% termination indemnity, if applicable. Staff members who received their abolishment notifications on or after 1 April 2025 may take on a temporary assignment in any duty station regardless of hardship classification, without losing any of the separation entitlements.

TempI already signed the agreement to receive the 50% additional termination indemnity. May I still apply for temporary assignment?
▾

If you already signed the no-contest agreement to receive the 50% termination indemnity, to become eligible to go on temporary assignments in a non-hardship duty station, you need to revoke the no-contest agreement signed.

TempIf a Fixed Term appointment holder on abolished post takes up a Temporary Assignment, how will this affect the separation entitlements?
▾

If the staff member does not manage to secure a new Fixed Term post to be reassigned to upon conclusion of the Temporary Assignment, the staff member will separate from service. The termination indemnity/separation payment will be calculated based on the length of continuous service and contract end date at the time of separation. See the Knowledge Article: Staff on Abolished Post Taking Up a Temporary Assignment.

Frequently Asked Questions — Abolishment close to retirement
DeferralWhat is separation deferral? Who is eligible?
▾

Separation deferral is a special arrangement between certain separating staff members and the organization to bridge the gap between separation from service and retirement within two years of reaching early or normal retirement age. You are eligible if: your appointment is terminated for reasons of abolishment of post, reduction of staff or mutually agreed termination in the interest of the good administration of the organization AND within 2 years from the termination date you will reach your early or normal retirement age, OR complete 25 or 30 years of contributory service with the Pension Fund. Separation deferral is possible up to a maximum of two years.

DeferralWhat does the separation deferral look like in practice?
▾

Separation deferral delays the separation from service by maximum two years. During separation deferral, staff members are placed on Special Leave with Full Pay (SLWFP) for a period equal in cost to the staff member's total termination indemnity; then Special Leave Without Pay (SLWOP) if the SLWFP is not sufficient to bridge the gap to early or normal retirement age. Once approved, termination indemnity will be paid out over a period of time as full monthly salary including allowances, after deducting the staff member's contributions to UNJSPF, medical insurance and life insurance. During SLWOP, the staff member does not receive salaries or allowances and is responsible for full pension contributions (both individual and organization share) as well as health and life insurance payments. Periods of SLWFP granted for separation deferral are solely for pension purposes and will not be counted as part of service for calculating separation entitlements and benefits.

DeferralHow and when should I submit my request for separation deferral?
▾

Staff members on abolished posts are requested to notify their HR local focal point (LFP) about their interest in the separation deferral option as early as possible. The GSSC can provide the estimated duration of special leave with full pay period to assist the staff member in making the decision. The HR LFP will brief you about the option. If you are interested, the GSSC provides estimations on health insurance and pension fund contributions to be covered by you during the SLWOP period (if applicable). It is also highly recommended to visit the self-service site of UNJSPF to run simulations on your pension benefit based on different options.

DeferralWhy can separation deferral be a good option?
▾

If you are eligible, the separation deferral option offers the following advantages:

  • You may bridge the gap (max. 2 years) between your termination date and your early or normal retirement age.
  • You will separate at the end of the deferral period and be able to claim an early or normal retirement benefit from the Pension Fund (depending on your age).
  • If you meet all ASHI eligibility criteria, you may also apply for ASHI upon separation at the end of the deferral period — provided you continue contributing towards your health insurance plan throughout the deferral period.
DeferralI opted for separation deferral. When will I receive my final payment?
▾

If you opted for separation deferral, you would separate at the end of the separation deferral period. Your separation-related entitlements (cash compensation of your annual leave balance, remaining balance of termination indemnity, etc. as applicable) will be paid at that time.

DeferralWhat if I am eligible for separation deferral but I am not interested?
▾

Eligible staff may apply for separation deferral, but this is optional. If you are not interested, you will separate on the termination date and the applicable benefits will be paid to you.

DeferralWhat are the pros and especially the cons of the separation deferral for retirement purposes?
▾

The separation deferral is meant to support the staff member in being able to retire upon separation or reach the next milestone such as normal retirement age or 25/30 years of pension contribution, so that the pension benefit is a more advantageous one. However, in case the termination indemnity is not enough to keep the staff member on SLWFP until the retirement date/milestone, a SLWOP period starts during which the staff member needs to cover their own and the organization's share of the pension contribution and health insurance contribution in case they wish to benefit from After Service Health Insurance (provided that the requirements are met).

DeferralIs it mandatory to pay my own and the organization's share of pension and health insurance during the SLWOP?
▾

It is mandatory to pay your own and the organization's share of the pension premiums during the SLWOP period. Contribution towards the health insurance plan is not mandatory, however in case you are eligible and would like to benefit from ASHI at the time of separation, the contribution (own + organization's share) must be kept active.

DeferralI am entitled to 12 months of termination indemnity. Does that mean that this can keep me on SLWFP for 12 months?
▾

No. 1 month of termination indemnity equals to 1 month of gross salary minus staff assessment, while 1 month of salary during SLWFP is a regular salary including allowances. Therefore, 1 month of termination indemnity is less than 1 month of salary.

DeferralHow will I know exactly how many months of SLWFP and SLWOP my termination indemnity can cover?
▾

In case you are eligible for separation deferral, please submit a separation deferral calculation request by submitting a General enquiry in Service Gateway. The GSSC will come back to you with the calculation.

DeferralI only need 3 months of deferral until I reach my normal retirement age, but I am entitled to 12 months of termination indemnity. What will my deferral look like?
▾

In this case you will be placed on 3 months of SLWFP during which you will receive your regular monthly salary with allowances, funded by your termination indemnity. Once the three months have elapsed, you will separate from service and take normal retirement. The remaining termination indemnity will be included in your separation package.

DeferralAm I eligible for Education Grant during the separation deferral?
▾

No, as the separation deferral is only a technical bridging and will not be counted as part of service for calculating separation entitlements and benefits.

Frequently Asked Questions — Reappointment after separation on post abolishment
ReappointmentDo I need to repay the termination indemnity if I am reappointed? What about the other separation related payments?
▾

Most separation payments do not need to be repaid, regardless of contract type (FT, TA, or consultancy):

  • Annual leave liquidation — no repayment required
  • CTO payment — no repayment required
  • Repatriation grant — no repayment required
  • Termination indemnity / separation payment — no repayment required

The only exception is the additional 50% termination indemnity:

  • Reappointed within 12 months on a fixed-term contract — must be repaid
  • Reappointed on a temporary appointment or consultancy — no repayment required
  • Reappointed beyond 12 months (any contract type) — no repayment required
ReappointmentI am receiving retirement benefit, and I have an ASHI plan. Will I lose these if I am reappointed?
▾

No, you will not lose these benefits. There are 2 major scenarios:

  • Scenario 1 — Contract ≤ 6 months and total calendar year UN income below USD 50,000: You will be able to keep your ongoing retirement benefit and ASHI coverage without interruption.
  • Scenario 2 — Exceeding the above limits: Both your retirement benefit and ASHI will be suspended for the duration of your new contract. You will contribute to the pension fund and be covered by a UNICEF medical plan. Upon separation, your retirement benefit will resume and you may request re-activation of your ASHI plan (with unchanged conditions).
