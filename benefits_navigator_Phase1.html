Important notes for all staff
Read before and during your separation process
Settle all debts before separation
Outstanding salary advance, rental advance, education grant advance, or other cash advances must be cleared with your People & Culture Business Partner or Finance Focal Point. UNJSPF will not take action on your pension benefits until all debts vis-à-vis UNICEF are settled. Ensure your office clears your local PAR Account prior to departure. Separation payments will be remitted towards any outstanding loans with UNFCU, La Mutuelle or equivalent financial institutions in accordance with Section 6 of ST/AI/2000/12.
Pension (UNJSPF)
Obtain your UID by emailing requestuidonly@unjspf.org, then log in at member.unjspf.org. For your UNJSPF Annual Statement, visit the UNJSPF Member Self-Service portal.
Health insurance
Your CIGNA/Aetna coverage expires at the end of your last month of employment — not your last working day.
ASHI & ASLI
After-Service Health and Life Insurance eligibility depends on meeting retirement criteria and receiving a monthly pension benefit. Verify through the ASHI Process Instruction and the ASLI Process Instruction.

Annual leave & CTO
LeaveWill I receive compensation for all annual leave days I did not use?
▾

Yes. Upon separation you will receive compensation for unused annual leave days. The maximum is 18 days for Temporary Appointments and 60 days for FT/Continuing/Permanent contracts. Any leave beyond 60 days will be forfeited — you may wish to take excess leave before separation, subject to supervisor approval.

Payment is automatic — included in your final payment approximately 4–6 weeks after your separation date.

LeaveCan I take annual leave in the last month before separation?
▾

Yes, subject to approval from your supervisor.

LeaveWho is entitled to CTO compensation upon separation?
▾

Only General Service (GS) staff accrue CTO. IP and National Officer staff do not. Upon separation, GS staff may receive compensation for up to 40 CTO hours plus CTO accrued during the last 4 months of employment. Excess CTO is forfeited.

LeaveWhat is the in lieu of notice payment and do I need to claim it?
▾

FT/Continuing/Permanent: 6 months' written notice is required. If not possible, cash compensation equivalent to the shortfall is paid. TA staff: 15 days' notice required; cash compensation for any shortfall. You do not need to claim it — the GSSC Offboarding team includes it automatically in your final payment.

Travel & shipment
TravelWho is entitled to repatriation travel and shipment?
▾

International Professional staff serving outside their home country and appointed there at UNICEF's cost are entitled. Eligible family members (spouse and dependent children) installed at UNICEF's cost also qualify, unless you are on a TA or at a non-family duty station.

TravelWhat are my shipment options?
▾

TA: 100 kg by air + USD 1,600 insurance or USD 1,500 relocation grant in lieu.
FT/Cont/Perm (non-JPO): 20-foot container (no dependants) or 40-foot (with dependants) + USD 16,000 insurance or USD 13,000 / USD 18,000 relocation grant.
JPO: 600 kg + 250 kg (first family) + 150 kg (each additional) or USD 9,000 / USD 12,000 relocation grant.

TravelWhat is the repatriation grant and who is eligible?
▾

A one-time lump sum to assist re-establishment outside the country of the last duty station. You are eligible if you have at least 5 years of qualifying service, are residing outside your country of nationality/home leave/permanent residence at separation, and relocate away from the duty station. You must claim it within 2 years of separation — it is not paid automatically.

Termination indemnity & separation payment
IndemnityMy post is abolished. Will I receive termination indemnity?
▾

Contract valid beyond abolishment date: Yes — termination indemnity applies regardless of length of service. No No Contest Agreement required.
Contract expiring on the same day as abolishment: No termination indemnity. However, if you have at least 5 years of continuous service and sign the No Contest Agreement, you receive a separation payment equivalent to termination indemnity.

IndemnityHow is termination indemnity calculated when I have less than 6 years of service?
▾

Each full month of uncompleted service = 1 week of termination indemnity. Minimum: 6 weeks. Maximum: 3 months of net salary. This applies to all TA staff and to FT staff with under 6 years of service whose contract runs beyond the abolishment date.

IndemnityHow is termination indemnity calculated when I have 6 or more years of service?
▾

Full termination indemnity is calculated per Annex 1 of DHR/PROCEDURE/2022/002 — ranging from 3 months (at 6 years) up to 12 months (at 15+ years) for FT contracts; and from 6 months to 12 months for Continuing/Permanent contracts. Both years and months of service are counted, not just full years.

IndemnityCan I receive the additional 50% termination indemnity?
▾

Only if you received your abolishment notification before 1 April 2025. Signing the No Contest Agreement for the additional 50% is optional and means you withdraw pending claims against UNICEF. If you received notification on or after 1 April 2025, you are not eligible. Note: applying for SLWOP means forfeiting the additional 50%.

IndemnityWhen will I receive my termination indemnity or separation payment?
▾

It is paid automatically as part of your final payment, approximately 4–6 weeks after your separation date. You do not need to claim it.

Pension (UNJSPF)
PensionWhat pension benefits are available upon separation?
▾

Benefits depend on your contribution period and age. Under 5 years: withdrawal settlement only. At 5+ years below early retirement age: withdrawal settlement or deferred retirement benefit. At early retirement age: also early retirement benefit. At normal retirement age: normal retirement benefit. For estimates, visit the UNJSPF Member Self-Service portal.

PensionWhen may I retire and what are the retirement ages?
▾

Normal retirement age: 60 (contributions before 1 Jan 1990), 62 (1990–2013), or 65 (on/after 1 Jan 2014). Early retirement age: 55 (before 1 Jan 2014) or 58 (on/after 1 Jan 2014). Both require at least 5 years of contributions.

PensionHow long does it take to receive my pension benefit?
▾

The GSSC submits your documents to the Pension Fund close to your separation date. If all documents are in order and you have no outstanding debts, the Pension Fund releases your benefit approximately 4–6 weeks after your last month's salary. Contact: Contact Us — UNJSPF.

PensionCan I receive both termination indemnity and pension for the same period?
▾

Yes, until the age of 65. Once you turn 65, only pension payment may apply.

ASHI & ASLI
ASHIAm I eligible for After-Service Health Insurance (ASHI)?
▾

You must meet all of: permanent/continuing/FT appointment at separation; at least 10 years of UN health plan contributions (5 years if contributing before July 2007, excluding TA periods); already reached early retirement age; will receive a monthly pension benefit; apply within 31 days before or after separation. ASHI cannot be activated later if not applied at separation. See ASHI Process Instruction.

ASLIAm I eligible for After-Service Life Insurance (ASLI)?
▾

You are automatically enrolled if at least 55 years old at separation, participated in Group Life Insurance for at least 10 years, and have an active life insurance plan at separation. Under 55 with 10+ years: eligible for 1 year of free coverage per completed 10 years. Contact hlis@un.org. See ASLI Process Instruction.

ASHII am opting for deferred retirement benefit. Can I still take ASHI?
▾

Yes, if eligible at separation. You will pay ASHI contributions directly until you receive a monthly pension benefit, at which point premiums are deducted from your pension automatically.

Early separation
SeparationMy post is abolished. May I separate before the abolishment date?
▾

Yes — this is called early separation. Submit a written request (not a resignation) to your supervisor: FT = 1 month notice, Cont/Perm = 3 months, TA = 15 days. Approval is not automatic — the Head of Office decides. If approved, all separation entitlements are preserved.

SeparationWill early separation affect my termination indemnity amount?
▾

Notification before 1 April 2025: No effect — calculated as if you remained until the abolishment date.
Notification on/after 1 April 2025: Calculated based on your actual separation date, not the abolishment date.

SeparationIs early separation different from resignation?
▾

Yes — they are completely different. Resigning means you lose your termination indemnity and separation payment. Early separation is a separation on post abolishment before the abolishment date, and all entitlements remain. Always ask for "early separation" and never submit a resignation.

SLWOP & temporary assignment
SLWOPWho is eligible for SLWOP and how long can it last?
▾

Staff receiving notice on or before 31 December 2025 may request up to 12 months of SLWOP. Apply at least 2 months in advance. You retain internal candidate status and can keep health insurance active by paying both your own and the organisation's contribution. Separation entitlements are paid at actual separation.

SLWOPWho is eligible for a temporary assignment and what are the limits?
▾

Staff on abolished post status selected for an IP temporary appointment starting before 31 December 2025. Non-hardship duty stations: max 364 days. Hardship duty stations: max 729 days (must complete 6 months before applying elsewhere). A staff member who completes a temporary assignment is ineligible for SLWOP.

Abolishment close to retirement & reappointment
RetirementWhat is separation deferral and am I eligible?
▾

A bridging arrangement for staff within 2 years of early/normal retirement age (or 25/30 years of pension contributions) at abolishment. You are placed on Special Leave with Full Pay (SLWFP) funded by your termination indemnity, followed by SLWOP if needed. During SLWOP you must cover both your own and the organisation's pension and health insurance contributions. Notify your local P&C focal point as early as possible.

RetirementDo I need to repay termination indemnity if I am reappointed?
▾

No — termination indemnity and separation payment do not need to be repaid upon reappointment. The only exception is the additional 50% termination indemnity: if reappointed within 12 months, this must be repaid. Beyond 12 months — no repayment required.
